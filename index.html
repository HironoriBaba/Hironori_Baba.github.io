Yamaha Corporation

Mechanical Design Engineering Intern

(June - September 2018)

I worked on Yamaha's next generation electric keyboard!

Specifically, I invented a keyboard that is shorter than 30% compared to previous models using a linkage mechanism. This size reduction methodology is special, because I also improved how the keyboard feels to a performer (usually size and feel is a tradeoff). To verify my design, I developed a semantic differential survey to gather feedbacks from other mechanical engineers at Yamaha, and assessed the results. The design is now being considered for a patent by Yamaha, and I helped brainstorm for the company's internal patent application. In the meantime, I also learned about how to design for injection molding, felt a lot of keyboards, and played baseball with my coworkers!
